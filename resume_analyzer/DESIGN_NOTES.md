# Design plan (internal notes)

Subject: a resume-scanning desk tool. Audience: students/job-seekers, final-year
portfolio reviewers. Page job: make uploading + reading an ATS-style analysis feel
authoritative and a little tactile — like a document being reviewed and stamped.

Palette (avoiding cream+terracotta and near-black+neon defaults):
- Paper:      #EFEAE0 (cool-leaning paper, not the #F4F1EA cliché)
- Ink:        #1C2541 (deep navy ink — headings, primary text)
- Highlighter:#FFD23F (highlighter yellow — matched skill tags)
- Pen-red:    #C1443B (red-pen correction accent — flags/weak phrases only)
- Stamp-green:#2F9E68 (approved stamp — high scores)
- Muted-teal: #3E7C7A (links, secondary accents)

Type:
- Display: Space Grotesk (geometric, confident, not the serif-editorial cliché)
- Body: Inter
- Utility/data: IBM Plex Mono (scores, percentages, section labels — "scanned data" feel)

Layout/signature: hero styled like a document on a light table with a horizontal
"scanner beam" sweeping down over a stylized resume card on load. Results page uses
a circular stamped score gauge (conic-gradient) rather than a generic progress bar,
and matched skills render as literal highlighter-marker tags.

Structural devices: left-margin "punch holes" on cards evoke printed paper without
going full broadsheet/hairline-only. Rounded stamp shapes contrast the grid to keep
it from reading as newspaper template.
